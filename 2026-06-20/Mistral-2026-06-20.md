## **1. What are the greatest challenges facing humanity today?**

**Climate Change & Ecological Collapse**
The most existential threat is anthropogenic climate change, with global temperatures already ~1.2°C above pre-industrial levels. The IPCC's 2023 AR6 Synthesis Report confirms that current policies put us on track for **~2.7–3.1°C warming by 2100**, triggering catastrophic feedback loops: permafrost thaw (releasing methane), Amazon dieback, and ice sheet collapse. Biodiversity loss—**1 million species at risk of extinction** (IPBES 2019)—compounds this, threatening food systems and ecosystem stability.

**Geopolitical Fragmentation & Conflict**
The post-Cold War order is collapsing. Russia’s 2022 invasion of Ukraine, the Israel-Hamas war, and rising US-China tensions (Taiwan, South China Sea) risk **direct great-power conflict**. The 2024 *Global Peace Index* ranked the world at its **least peaceful since 2008**, with **112 armed conflicts** active in 2023. Nuclear saber-rattling (Russia, North Korea) and AI-driven disinformation exacerbate instability.

**Inequality & Economic Precarity**
Global wealth inequality is extreme: the **richest 1% own 43% of global assets** (Credit Suisse 2023), while **10% of the world lives on <$2.15/day** (World Bank). Automation and AI threaten **30% of jobs** (McKinsey 2023), risking mass unemployment without policy intervention. The **cost-of-living crisis** (2022–2024) pushed **700M+ into poverty** (UNDP).

**Technological Disruption & Misalignment**
AI capabilities are advancing faster than governance. **Frontier models** (e.g., LLMs, autonomous weapons) risk **loss of control** (alignment problem), mass surveillance, and deepfake-driven societal manipulation. The **2023 AI Safety Summits** highlighted gaps in global coordination on AI risks.

**Pandemic & Global Health Risks**
COVID-19 killed **7M+** (WHO 2024) and exposed fragility in global health systems. **Antimicrobial resistance (AMR)** could cause **10M deaths/year by 2050** (O’Neill Report). Zoonotic spillover risks (e.g., avian flu H5N1 in 2024–2026) remain high due to deforestation and industrial farming.

**Other Critical Challenges**
- **Food & Water Insecurity:** 2023–2024 saw **El Niño-driven droughts** in Africa, reducing harvests by **30–40%** (FAO). **258M people** faced acute food insecurity in 2023 (WFP).
- **Democratic Backsliding:** **72% of the world’s population** now live in **autocratizing** or **authoritarian** regimes (V-Dem Institute 2024).
- **Misinformation:** **56% of countries** experienced **foreign disinformation campaigns** in 2023 (Freedom House).

### **1. How close are we to curing cancer?**

**Current Status (2026):**
- **Not a single "cure," but a collection of treatments** for different cancers.
- **5-Year Survival Rates (US, 2024):**
  - **Prostate:** 99%
  - **Breast:** 91%
  - **Melanoma:** 94% (thanks to **immunotherapy**)
  - **Lung:** 25% (but **68% for early-stage** with new therapies)
  - **Pancreatic:** 12% (still deadly)

**Breakthroughs:**

| **Therapy**               | **Mechanism**                          | **Success Stories**                                                                                     | **Limitations**                                                                                     |
|---------------------------|----------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Immunotherapy**         | Boosts immune system to attack tumors  | **Jimmy Carter (2015):** Melanoma remission; **Keytruda (Merck)** – $25B/year, 30+ cancer types     | Works for **~20–30% of patients**, can cause autoimmune side effects                              |
| **CAR-T Cell Therapy**    | Genetically engineered T-cells          | **90% remission in some blood cancers** (e.g., leukemia, lymphoma) – **$400K–$1M per treatment**     | Only for **blood cancers**, severe side effects (cytokine release syndrome)                     |
| **mRNA Vaccines**         | Prevents cancer by targeting mutations | **Moderna/Pfizer:** Personalized cancer vaccines in **Phase 3 trials (2026)** – early results: **50% reduction in melanoma recurrence** | Still experimental, high cost, requires **individualized production**                             |
| **Targeted Therapy**      | Drugs block specific cancer mutations | **Herceptin (breast cancer)**, **Gleevec (leukemia)** – **90%+ effectiveness for some cancers**        | **Resistance develops**, only works for **cancers with known mutations**                          |
| **CRISPR Gene Editing**   | Edits cancer-causing genes             | **First human trials (2026):** Early success in **leukemia** (China, US)                               | **Off-target effects**, ethical concerns, long-term safety unknown                              |
| **AI for Early Detection**| Machine learning for diagnostics       | **Google DeepMind:** 90% accuracy in **breast cancer screening**; **PathAI:** Reduces misdiagnosis by 50% | **Bias in datasets**, false positives, privacy concerns                                           |

**Timeline to "Cure":**

- **2026–2030:** **Personalized cancer vaccines** (mRNA-based) become standard for **melanoma, lung, breast cancer**.
- **2030–2035:** **CAR-T 2.0** (off-the-shelf, cheaper) expands to **solid tumors**.
- **2035–2040:** **AI-driven drug discovery** (e.g., AlphaFold 3) leads to **universal cancer treatments** for major types.
- **2040+:** **"Cure" for 80% of cancers** (early detection + combination therapies), but **some rare/aggressive cancers remain incurable**.

**Biggest Obstacles:**
